Engine Revision Notes

Vec3

Vec3(x, y, z)

Represents a point in 3D space.

Stores:

- x
- y
- z

Example:

Vec3(1, 2, 3)

---

Triangle

Triangle(p1, p2, p3)

Represents one triangle face.

Contains three Vec3 points.

Example:

Triangle(
    Vec3(0, 0, 0),
    Vec3(1, 0, 0),
    Vec3(0, 1, 0)
)

---

Mesh

Mesh(vertices, faces)

Represents the geometry of an object.

Contains:

- vertices
- faces

Contains no position.

Contains no rotation.

Examples:

- Box
- Cube
- Sphere
- Gun Mesh

---

Transform

Transform(x, y, z)

Represents where something is and how it is rotated.

Contains:

- x
- y
- z
- rx
- ry
- rz

Contains no geometry.

---

Object3D

Object3D(mesh, transform)

Combines:

Mesh
+
Transform

Represents a thing that exists in the world.

Examples:

- Barrel
- Stock
- Grip
- Cube

---

Model

Model()

Contains multiple Object3Ds.

Used to build larger objects from smaller parts.

Examples:

- Gun
- Car
- Character

Contains its own Transform.

Allows all child Object3Ds to move and rotate together.

---

world_transform()

world_transform(model, obj)

Combines:

Model Transform
+
Object Transform
=
World Transform

Produces the final transform of an Object3D in world space.

---

Camera

Camera()

Represents the player's view of the world.

Contains:

Transform

Moving the camera does not move objects.

Instead, the world is transformed relative to the camera.

---

camera_transform()

camera_transform(camera, world_transform)

Converts:

World Space
↓
Camera Space

Applies the inverse of the camera transform.

---

Scene

Scene()

Contains multiple Models.

Examples:

Gun
Tree
House
Enemy

The renderer draws everything inside the Scene.

---

project()

project(point)

Converts:

3D
↓
2D

using perspective division.

Example:

x = point.x / point.z
y = point.y / point.z

---

Renderer

render(camera, scene, screen, width, height)

Draws every model in the scene.

Responsible for:

World Transform
↓
Camera Transform
↓
Projection
↓
Drawing
